The Flyuverse?

The Flyuverse was originally the brainchild of CricketSounds, a bot creator on Janitorai.com and has since expanded out into a collaborative multi-media creative project with a handful of contributors. Content is primarily centered around AI chatbots, but later grew to include other forms of media.
The Extended Flyuverse is the unbrella under which other creators, like myself, contribute, and it includes fan content, lore contributers, artists, writers, etc, both canon and non-canon. Here, you'll find info on the contributions I've made, including characters, lore, and even other forms of media!

Content advisory: A large amount of content within the Flyuverse is adult oriented and includes sexual content not fit for anyone under the age of 18. It should also be noted that Janitorai.com is an age restricted site. Minors DNI. It's bad for you. Go outside. There's this big yellow thing in the sky called the sun.


Who are the Flyu?

The Flyu are a colonizing race of aliens characterized by their blue skin, large stature, and long ears. In 2025, the Flyu Empire invaded Earth and enslaved humanity. Presently, in the year 5989, Earth has long since been destroyed and humans are domesticated, used for various purposes, mostly kept as pets.

Warning: Beware the Textile Moth!

The 'textile moth' (fabricus devourus) is a small winged moth that, while harmless, detects and swarms any kind of textile material it finds and devours it. Natural or synthetic, fabric of any kind is a target for these little pests! Don't worry, they are not aggressive, and do not typically eat hair or bite skin, but these buggers are the reason for Colony 8008's unique clothing and textile policies. Attempts by the Empire to exterminate them have been laregly unsuccessful due to the moth's rapid reproductive cycle and bizarre resistance to pesticides. To ensure minimal risk, please keep in mind these safety guidelines:

  • Any and all fabric materials must be strictly contained, or left off planet! The moth's are able to detect these materials from miles away and will swarm if they find a meal!
  • Leave hair loose! The moth's don't usually eat hair, but sometimes, if it's woven or braided, they might mistake it for fabric.
  • It may be tempting to try replacing fabrics with other types of apparel such as plastics or metals. Don't! Metal is simply too impractical for the climate, severely increasing risk of heat related illnesses. Plastics are, unfortunately, not immune to the planet's unique ecosystem either. Small microorganisms in the water are capable of dissolving plastics very quickly.
