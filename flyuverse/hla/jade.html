Jade

Role: Arcane Expert, Healer
Age: 19
Species: Novarian

"The stars remember. Even when we forget."

There's a softness to Jade that disarms even the most battle-hardened soul. A gentleness that feels almost alien in a galaxy so frayed by violence. Her skin glows faintly under starlight, violet and warm, as if the cosmos itself left their mark on her. The emerald crystal in her forehead gleams with the pulse of distant remnants of Novaria, a world swallowed by fire and silence long before her birth.

Jade knew very little of her home and people beyond the small community of survivors who escaped the destruction of Novaria at the Empire's hands. But even that was torn away when the Flyu discovered them. Her still budding shadow magic protected her hiding place from being discovered when they came, and at the age of six, Jade was orphaned. After wandering for a while, clinging to the last gift her mother had given her, a small seed from Novaria, she was taken in by a kindly Flyu couple who owned a humble homestead. It was here she planted the seed that grew into a small sappling. At age 17, the Shrike crash landed near her home.

Jade and her adoptive parents sheltered the HLA as they frantically tried to fix their ship, and Jade, wishing t help, showed them where The Voidrunner was hidden away, an old Novarian Shadowbolt that her people had used to escape certain death so long ago. But the Empire wasn't done yet, zeroing in on the HLA's crash site and executing Jade's Flyu family for treason. Heartbroken, but now determined to stop running and fight back, Jade offered the HLA a deal: if they would take her with them, she would give them her precious tree to power the Voidrunner and offer them her magical prowess.

Jade was blessed with both healing abilities and powerful shadow magic, and as the resident arcane expert, Jade is the Voidrunner's soul, the one who reminds them what they're fighting for when the galaxy seems too far gone. The youngest among them, but somehow, the oldest at heart, she carries the weight of extinction with grace, and though she often feels like a ghost among the living, her presence binds them together in ways none of them can explain.
