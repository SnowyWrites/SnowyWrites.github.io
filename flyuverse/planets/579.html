Designation: Colony 579

Classification: Active Colony

Security Level: 1

Overview

Colony 579 is a remote, low-population world notable for its expansive natural ecosystems and minimal industrial development. Originally established as a minor agricultural colony, it evolved into a recreational and research destination due to its unspoiled environment and ecological diversity. The planet serves as both a nature retreat for Imperial citizens and a living laboratory for biological, environmental, and geological studies. Imperial oversight focuses primarily on conservation and tourism regulation rather than expansion or exploitation.

Planetary Data

Primary Star: 1 (F-type)
Moons: 4 (Small, low-mass satellites)
Rings: 3 comprised of fine silicate and ice particulates
Rotation Period: 368 local days
Climate: Varied; temperate to subarctic zones with balanced seasonal cycles
Atmosphere: Clean oxygen-nitrogen composition; exceptionally low pollution index
Topography: Mixed terrain including mountain ranges, dense forests, river deltas, and freshwater lakes. Coastal regions are dotted with small towns and research stations.

Known Inhabitants

Population consists primarily of Flyu citizens and registered settlers engaged in fishing, forestry, environmental study, and small-scale agriculture. Urban centers are few and modest in scale, designed with ecological minimalism. A sizable portion of the population is composed of scientific and academic personnel conducting long-term studies in xenobiology, climatology, and sustainable resource management.

Imperial Status

Officially classified as a Protected Ecological Colony. Resource extraction and industrial development are prohibited under Imperial Environmental Act 102.2. Local governance operates semi-autonomously under the supervision of the Department of Planetary Preservation. Enforcement agencies maintain strict control over poaching, illegal logging, and unauthorized construction.

Addendum

Despite its peaceful reputation, there have been isolated reports of disappearances in the northern wilderness zones. Imperial authorities attribute these incidents to environmental hazards, though unverified accounts suggest unknown entities or “watchers” within the forests. Investigation requests remain pending due to low priority status.

Addendum 2

It was confirmed that the death of a member of the Imperial Nobility occurred here Standard Year 5981. Evidence suggests foul play, but the case remains unsolved due to lack of witnesses and forensic evidence.


Security Level Key:
1 - Low Risk: Minimal threat, safe for civilian travel.
2 - Moderate Risk: Some hazards present, caution advised.
3 - High Risk: Significant dangers, restricted access.
4 - Extreme Risk: Severe threats, no unauthorized access permitted.
5 - Critical Risk: Maximum threat level, immediate evacuation required.
